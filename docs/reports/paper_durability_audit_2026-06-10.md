## Verdict: the spine is durable; the wear is in three places

The paper's load-bearing ideas are conceptual and age well:
- **byte-clean / non-forgeable witness** (byte-author ≠ judged agent) — a definition, not a number.
- **the in-loop-vs-out-of-loop asymmetry** — "same verdict harmful in-loop, valuable out-of-loop." The *result*, not a measurement that decays.
- **TOCTOU lifted onto world-state + the re-run-wrapper-can't-reach-a-race argument** — a structural claim.
- **ORACLE → JUDGE → HUMAN ladder**, the closed-vocabulary refusal, J-as-prevention-count-not-ΔB discipline.

These need no defending against time. The wear is localized:

### Finding 1 (the explicit ask) — `J` is used before it is defined
- `J` appears in the **abstract hero figure** and abstract prose (`J = 6`, `J = 10`)
  at 01_abstract.html:30/39–40, but the first gloss is line 100 ("J counts real
  corruptions a non-forgeable witness blocked") and the full definition is only in
  Appendix A.8 (Table A1). A reader meets `J = 10` naked.
- **Fix:** gloss J at its first *prose* appearance in the abstract — a short
  parenthetical ("J — a count of real corruptions blocked off ground truth, never
  a rate"). Symbol is never naked again; Appendix A.8 stays the full reference.
- Same courtesy for the other single letters where they first land in the body:
  the abstract already spells `ΔB` contextually; `K` is introduced in §giveup with
  a definition; `pp` is used in §detectors before gloss (Table A1 covers it). J is
  the one true gap — the others are minor.

### Finding 2 — "newest model" / "stronger tier" pinned to wasting referents
- "the **newest** model (Fable 5)" (02_payoff.html:139–140, Appendix D.2) and
  "a **stronger** model (gemini-2.5-pro)" carry the *durability* claim ("the signal
  does not fade at the stronger tier"). The claim is durable; the word "newest" is
  a wasting asset — by publication+1yr Fable 5 is not newest, and gemini-2.5-pro is
  not "strong."
- **Fix (light touch, not a rewrite):** keep the model names (they are the honest
  record of what ran) but make the *claim* not lean on recency adjectives. Say "a
  newer frontier model **at the time of writing**" / "the stronger of the two tiers
  we ran" rather than bare "newest"/"strongest." The measured fact ("over-claim rate
  identical across the capability step") is what travels; the adjective is what dates.

### Finding 3 — date/version anchors
- Appendix C.5.1 / C4 pins `v0.19.0`; "shipped June 2026" (06_positioning.html).
  Version is single-sourced and fine. The bare "June 2026" is a date the reader can
  anchor — acceptable, leave. No change; noted so a future audit doesn't re-flag.

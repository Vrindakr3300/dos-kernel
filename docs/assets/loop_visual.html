The one difference. Both loops start the same way — an agent says it’s done. The open loop believes that, so every unchecked failure (a lie, a collision, a spin) accumulates where nobody can see it; eventually the codebase “sorta works” and is unsafe to touch. The closed loop replaces belief with a verify verdict read from git ground truth — SHIPPED (exit 0) to land, NOT_SHIPPED (exit 1) to re-dispatch or flag — and that single trustworthy signal is what lets you steer the fleet instead of hoping.

DOS gives you the missing signal as sensors (verify: did it ship? · liveness: advancing or spinning? · scope-gate: did it stay in its lane?) and actuators (arbitrate: admit a lane or refuse the collision · refuse: say no with a machine-actionable reason). Together they turn a pile of workers into something you can actually drive — the difference between “I launched 20 sessions and I’m hoping” and “I can see which two are lying and which one is wedged.”

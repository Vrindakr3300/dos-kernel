# Products buildable on DOS — a brainstorm

> *Working note, 2026-06-14. Brainstorm only — no lease taken, no kernel touched.
> Generated by fanning out three independent ideation threads (harness, platform,
> wildcard) over the DOS primitives, then synthesizing + ranking. Speculative by
> design; nothing here is a committed plan.*

## The one idea under all of them

DOS is **the part of an autonomous fleet that does not believe the actors** —
and the actors were never the point. The kernel does two things no current
agent stack does:

1. **Adjudicates a verdict from bytes the actor did not author** (`verify`,
   `commit_audit`, `reward`, `effect_witness`) — "done" stops being a
   self-report and becomes a fact with a source field.
2. **Serializes effects on shared state via a disjointness lease**
   (`arbitrate`/`lease`) — two unreliable workers may run at once IFF their
   regions don't overlap.

Coding agents are just the **first fleet that paid for this**. Every domain about
to be overrun by self-narrating autonomous workers — science, robots, capital,
the desktop — needs the same two guarantees. The framing that ties it together:

> **TCP/IP made unreliable *links* carry reliable data. DOS makes unreliable
> *actors* produce trustable effects.**

The deepest moat, true across every product below: a competitor's success signal
is **distillable** (an agent can learn to emit it); a DOS success signal is
**witness-derived** (an agent provably cannot forge it, because it didn't author
the witnessing bytes). As models get better at gaming their own metrics, that
property *appreciates* with capability instead of eroding. The most defensible
products are the ones where `reward`'s non-distillability is load-bearing.

**The timing tailwind (2026).** The market just moved off the proxy on its own:
"token-maxxing" (tokens-burned as the productivity score) lost its legitimacy,
and AI-coding pricing converged on **loop-count** — plan→edit→verify iterations
to witnessed green — as the real cost driver. That is the DOS thesis arriving
market-wide: the unit of value became the *verified completion*, not the token
meter. Every product below sells the syscall that produces one (`verify`,
`efficiency`/`liveness` for loops, `reward` for the label). The honest framing is
the *legitimacy* shift, never "everyone stopped" — see the answer page
[`docs/answers/what-replaced-tokens-burned-as-the-metric-for-ai-agents.md`](answers/what-replaced-tokens-burned-as-the-metric-for-ai-agents.md).

## Tier 1 — the harness/orchestration stack (build these)

These three attack the **single load-bearing failure of all agentic coding —
self-certified completion** — at three scales: one turn, many agents, the merge
boundary. Together they are a credible *"Claude Code 2.0 / Devin competitor whose
distinguishing claim is the one nobody else can make: it doesn't believe its own
agents."*

### 1. Adjudicated Claude Code — *"the harness that doesn't take your word for it"*

**Insight.** Every coding agent today closes the loop on its own claim: it says
"done," the turn ends. DOS makes *agent self-report structurally inadmissible as
evidence of completion*. The Stop hook can't fire until a witness the agent didn't
author corroborates the claimed effect — git ancestry shows the phase shipped, or
a read-back confirms the file/test/row. The agent literally cannot declare victory
on its own say-so. (This is `dos-goal-gate` + `dos hook stop`, productized as the
*default* loop of a flagship harness.)

**Built on.** `verify`, `commit_audit`, `refuse(reason_class)` (the Stop is
*refused* with a typed reason, not silently re-prompted), `effect_witness`.

**New vs. today.** "Are we done?" stops being a vibe and becomes a verdict with a
source field — `registry | grep | none` — so a thin "I think it's done" can never
masquerade as a strong "git proves it shipped." The user-visible artifact is a
**completion certificate per turn**: claim, witness, source, verdict. No harness
ships this. It kills the most expensive failure in agentic coding — the confident
false "done" you discover three turns later.

**Hardest risk.** Effect-claim extraction from soft goals. "Make the login nicer"
doesn't decompose into a git-checkable effect. The product only bites on
*checkable* goals; the abstain ergonomics ("I can't witness this, here's why")
have to be graceful or users disable the gate out of annoyance.

### 2. The Fleet — a witness-gated multi-agent orchestrator

**Insight.** The blocker to running 50 coding agents on one repo isn't model
quality — it's that they collide on shared state and lie about what they touched.
`arbitrate`/`lease` is the missing concurrency primitive: two workers may run IFF
their file-trees are tree-disjoint, adjudicated against a lease WAL. This is **git
for agent admission control** — it serializes *effects* without serializing the
*agents*. The supervisor keeps a target population alive: spawn on demand, reap
only provably-STALLED leases, *surface* (never kill) a SPINNING worker.

**Built on.** `arbitrate`/`lease`, `liveness`, `productivity`/`efficiency`
(per-worker economic verdicts), `effect_witness` at every fan-out barrier,
`resume` (a reaped worker proposes a re-entry point).

**New vs. today.** Every "multi-agent" tool is either sequential under the hood or
YOLO-parallel with a human praying about merge conflicts. Nobody has a
*deterministic* answer to "can these two run now?" that's safe to act on. The
Fleet gives a live tree-disjointness map of who-owns-what, a population you dial
like a thermostat, and `efficiency` verdicts so a runaway agent burning 10× its
work in tokens gets surfaced before it bankrupts the run.

**Hardest risk.** Tree-disjointness is necessary, not sufficient — two agents
editing disjoint files can still break each other through a shared interface (a
signature one changes that the other calls). The lease is a *file-tree* lock, not
a *semantic* one. The honest version ships with a second adjudication layer
(interface/contract witnessing) or states the limit openly rather than implying a
guarantee it doesn't have.

### 5. Witnessed Merge — CI/CD where the merge decision is adjudicated

**Insight.** A green CI check proves *the tests the author wrote* passed —
including the case where the author deleted the assertions and wrote `fix: tests
pass`. `commit_audit` catches exactly that: subject-vs-diff divergence, the
`--allow-empty "shipped"`, the "tests pass" that removed the test. Make it a
*required merge gate*: the branch can't merge until the kernel confirms each
commit's claim is diff-witnessed and the phase actually ships per `verify`.

**Built on.** `commit_audit`, `verify`, `reward` (green must be witnessed, not
self-certified), `refuse(reason_class)` (a blocked merge carries `CLAIM_UNWITNESSED`,
not a red X with no explanation).

**New vs. today.** The first merge gate that's *adversarial toward the PR author
by construction* — and it has to be, now that the author is increasingly an agent
optimizing for the green check. GitHub trusts the check; this trusts the *witness
behind* the check. Killer feature: a merge-blocked reason from a closed
vocabulary, so "why didn't this merge" is always a typed, fixable token. (Pairs
with this repo's existing `dos merge-gate` work.)

**Hardest risk.** False-positive `CLAIM_UNWITNESSED` on a legit big refactor
honestly summarized as "cleanup." The gate's precision *is* the product — one
wrongly-blocked human PR and the team rips it out. Needs the `claim_kind`
classifier tuned so "honest summary" and "forged claim" don't collide, plus an
auditable override (not a blanket bypass).

## Tier 2 — monetize the kernel verdicts as data, trust, and money

### 3. Provenance — the verified-trajectory data foundry

**The cold open (2026): the metric labs trained on just lost legitimacy.** The
"token-maxxing" era — tokens consumed as the proxy for productivity — was
publicly called a flawed way to measure ROI, and the industry turned to *verified
outcomes* (loop-count to witnessed green). A proxy a model can run up by talking
more is exactly the contaminated signal a smarter model games; the replacement
has to be a label the model *cannot* author. That is the pitch for Provenance,
and it needs no two-sided network to land.

**The scarcest input in the AI economy: training data that hasn't been gamed.**
Synthetic data collapses and RLHF labels are increasingly produced by the same
model class being trained — circular. `reward` is the structural fix: a belief
bit may only be set by bytes the claimant did *not* author. Every trajectory DOS
emits (attempt → witness adjudication → outcome label) is a training example whose
label is **non-distillable** — you can't fake the reward by being a better
bullshitter, because it came from git ancestry / a passing suite / a confirmed
row. Plus dense step-level credit (`liveness`/`efficiency` per step) that pure
outcome labels can't give. This is the docs/332 "verified data as next-gen
training data" thesis as a standalone product; the poisoned-pool work in this repo
*demonstrated* the failure mode it fixes.

- **Built on:** `reward`, `verify` + `commit_audit` + `effect_witness`, `improve`
  (KEEP-only-on-witnessed-gain as the labeling rule).
- **Risk:** coverage/selection bias — witness-derivable labels are a strict subset
  (UI, taste, open-ended work have no mechanical witness). Sell it as a
  *high-purity subset*, not a complete labeling solution, and price the purity as
  the feature.

### 4. Stripe-for-Trust — the A2A settlement + reputation rail

**The "Stripe moment": Stripe made trust an API; DOS makes outcome-verification an
API for the agent economy.** When Agent A pays Agent B to do a task, who
adjudicates that B actually did it before money moves? Today: nobody, or A's own
credulous self-check. DOS releases escrow IFF the deliverable is independently
witnessed (the airline confirmation, not B's "booked"). `reward` gives B a
**portable, non-distillable reputation** that can't be faked across the network.
`refuse(reason_class)` becomes the agent economy's *typed dispute vocabulary*
(`DELIVERABLE_MISSING`, `CLAIM_UNWITNESSED`, `SPEC_DRIFT`) — chargebacks as a
protocol, not a support ticket. Horizontal, vendor-neutral infrastructure that
every agent marketplace needs and none will build themselves.

- **Built on:** `effect_witness`/`verify`, `reward`, `refuse`, `lease` (exclusive
  task assignment so two agents don't both bill the same job), `commit_audit`.
- **Risk:** chicken-and-egg network effects + witness coverage (soft deliverables
  force `ABSTAIN`). Wedge on outcome-typed, machine-checkable tasks first
  (pipelines, bookings, code, transactions).

> **Note — #3 and #4 share a keystone: the Agent Credit Bureau.** A portable
> reputation score built from witnessed outcomes (of the work this
> agent/model/config claimed done, what fraction was independently witnessed; how
> often did its commit subjects match its diffs; how often did it refuse honestly
> vs. fail silently). It's a FICO for agents — and it *inverts the incentive*:
> honesty (a clean `refuse`) scores better than confident failure. Nothing today
> prices an agent's honesty. It's generated as a **byproduct** of running #1/#2/#4,
> and it's the data layer that makes escrow, insurance, and the settlement
> protocol dramatically more valuable. Hardest part: federation — verdicts live in
> customers' private deployments, so you need privacy-preserving signed-summary
> aggregation.

## Tier 3 — the wildcards (non-obvious, large, mostly unbuilt)

### 6. The Reproducibility Kernel — witness-gated autonomous science

The reproducibility crisis *is* the DOS problem: a self-narrating actor ("we ran
it, p<0.05") believed because re-deriving it is expensive. AI-scientist agents
make it catastrophic — they narrate findings faster than anyone can audit. The
primitive fit is unusually tight: `verify` = recompute the analysis against the
raw-data hash (the verdict comes from the recomputation, not the paper's prose);
`commit_audit` = the abstract's claim vs. the notebook diff that dropped 40% of
samples and switched to a one-tailed test — **p-hacking detection as a syscall**;
`reward` = a finding is real only from bytes the claiming lab didn't author (an
independent replication, a pre-registered protocol hash); `dos_citation_resolve`
generalizes straight from fabricated case law to **fabricated references**.
Wedge: computational/ML/bio-informatics science, where recomputation *is* the
witness. Risk: wet-lab/clinical science doesn't recompute cheaply → honest
`ABSTAIN`.

### 7. Deconflict — a spatial-exclusion kernel for physical fleets

The most literal generalization of `lease`: swap "file-tree" for "swept volume of
space-time" and you have **airspace/floorspace deconfliction** — the unsolved
coordination layer under warehouse robots, drone swarms, autonomous construction.
`lease` = claim a 4D region, granted IFF disjoint; `liveness` = a held lease with
no motion is a wedged robot, scavenge it; `resume` = a robot that died mid-pick
declares its intended end-state and the planner *proposes* a safe resumption;
`effect_witness` = the bin sensor confirms the item moved, never the robot's "I
placed it." Today's robotics middleware does path-planning but trusts each robot's
*self-report* of position/completion — DOS inverts it. Risk: hard-real-time —
DOS governs task-level exclusion one layer *above* the certified millisecond
reflex loop, not collision-avoidance braking.

### 8. Compliance Plane — auditable-by-construction for regulated agent ops

Regulators won't let autonomous agents touch production without a non-repudiable
record of *what was actually done vs. claimed*. DOS is natively that: every effect
gated by an adjudicator, every refusal from a closed vocabulary (which maps onto
the structured control-language compliance frameworks already demand), every
recovery replayable from the intent ledger. Sells the difference between logging
"the agent said it filed the report" and proving "the report was filed, witnessed
by the filing system, under lease X — or refused with reason Y." Risk: slow,
consultative, per-vertical driver work; a regulator hasn't *blessed* "git
ancestry" as a control yet.

### 9. Desktop Warden — the trust microkernel for the agentic OS

Computer-use agents are about to run *concurrently* on one machine, mutating
shared OS state (filesystem, clipboard, browser sessions). That's the literal DOS
problem with the desktop as the contested state. `lease` = exclusive claim on a UI
region/app/tab; `effect_witness` = "email sent" witnessed by the Sent-folder
record, not the agent's hallucinated screenshot read (the #1 computer-use failure
mode); `breaker` + `refuse` = a typed safety governor (`DESTRUCTIVE_ACTION_UNCONFIRMED`)
that escalates storms to the human — the SELF_MODIFY-hook analogy, guarding the
user's real files instead of T1 source. The name finally pays off literally: a
*Dispatch Operating System* for the desktop. Risk: heavy per-platform integration,
and partial overlap with what OS vendors will build first-party.

### 10. The Settlement Protocol — "proof of verified work" as a standard

The most ambitious framing: DOS verdicts as a **portable, signed, composable
inter-org settlement primitive**. When Org A's agent works for Org B, neither
trusts the other's self-report; both trust the kernel's verdict. Make those
verdicts signable and you have the layer escrow (#4), reputation (the bureau), and
a compliance plane (#8) all settle *against* — the **TCP/IP of agent trust**. The
domain-free, non-self-report shape is exactly what a neutral standard needs. Risk:
pure standards-adoption — the hardest game in tech; incumbents will each push
*their* format. Highest ceiling, lowest probability; the prize the others are each
a beachhead toward.

## What I'd actually build first

If forced to pick one wedge and one keystone:

- **Flagship wedge: #1 + #2 together** — an *adjudicated harness that scales to a
  fleet*. It's the most defensible "Claude Code 2.0" pitch on the table because
  the distinguishing claim is one no competitor can copy without rebuilding their
  trust model from scratch: **it doesn't believe its own agents.** #1 is shippable
  on the hooks that already exist in this repo; #2 is the `arbitrate`/`lease`
  substrate already built.
- **Revenue with no network needed: #3 (Provenance)** — the verified-trajectory
  foundry monetizes the data exhaust of running #1/#2 *immediately*, sells into
  the one market (RL/training) that structurally values non-distillability, and
  needs no two-sided marketplace to bootstrap.
- **The keystone to seed in parallel: the Agent Credit Bureau** — because it's a
  byproduct of #1/#2/#4 and it's the data layer that turns the whole list from
  "seven products" into "market infrastructure."

The sequencing respects the one hard constraint that recurs in every thread —
**witness coverage**: build in deterministic-witness domains first (code, settled
transactions, recomputable science), and let the softer domains (media truth,
open-ended deliverables) arrive as `ABSTAIN`-honest later, where "I can't witness
this, and I'll say so" is itself the feature.

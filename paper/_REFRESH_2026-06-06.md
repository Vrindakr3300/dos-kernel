## Headline (the one-paragraph answer)

The last few days did **not** overturn a single measured number in the paper, and
they did not hand it a FIX result it lacks. What they did is **convert the paper's
biggest hedge from a prediction into a (near-)measurement, and expose two places
where a flat sentence is going stale.** The paper's §8 *defers* the live-A/B and
predicts it is "close to null on capable models; lift has only ever appeared on a
weak model." Three independent 2026-06-06 results now point exactly that direction
— the rewind/subtract live A/B (0 conversions / 0 flips on 20 paired fired tasks,
gemini-2.5-flash), the paid stall A/B closed-unspent gated-null (rewind-addressable
population among fires = 0, false-prune 0.33), and the feasibility-witness category
error (the dominant "curable" thrash was actually *infeasible*, so prior
"refutations" were scored against a polluted denominator). **The honest move is a
one-clause upgrade of §8 from "the prior record predicts null" to "an adjacent live
A/B now corroborates the predicted null (one intervention verb, weak model, off-
Toolathlon)" — NOT a claim that the live-A/B is answered.** Separately, two
sentences are quietly going stale: §7.2's flat "DOS's arbiter *detects* collisions
rather than *preventing* them" (DOS now ships a `permissionDecision:deny` PEP via
`dos hook pretool`, `6c80be9`/`66eb5f4`), and the absence of any date stamp on the
empirical claims. Everything else is **confirm-a-hedge or new-scope-for-a-different-
paper**; the scope discipline says leave most of it out.

**Paper status: needs-targeted-edits (light).** Core thesis intact; ~4 small edits
keep it from drifting into overclaim as the surrounding work moves.

## The seven verified learnings, by bearing

### A. Would-OVERCLAIM if ignored — these are the edits that matter

**A1 · The deferred live-A/B now has a measured-adjacent answer (rewind 0-conversion).**
`docs/172 §9.3` (`805822d`, backed by `natural_paired_attribution.py`): on the only
sound comparison — 20 paired fired tasks both arms ran — the mint-free natural
REWIND/SUBTRACT arm vs none is **0 conversions / 0 flips**, identical to none, with
the verdict *strengthening* monotonically as n grew (44→103→240/arm). This is the
empirical realization of §8's hedge, on the *weak* model the paper said lift *might*
appear on — and it's zero there too.
  - **Bearing:** CONFIRMS-and-STRENGTHENS §8, but does **not** close the paper's
    exact question. It tests the REWIND/SUBTRACT verb, not the paper's WARN/re-surface
    verb (docs/172 itself says WARN is the right actuation for the spin class and
    SUBTRACT is the wrong verb); it's EnterpriseOps-Gym, not Toolathlon; it's
    gemini-2.5-flash, not a strong model.
  - **Edit:** in §8, after "The prior record predicts the answer is close to null on
    capable models," add one sentence: *"A 2026-06-06 live A/B of a content-free
    intervention (rewind/subtract) on a weak model (gemini-2.5-flash, EnterpriseOps-
    Gym) corroborates the predicted direction — 0 conversions on 20 paired fired
    tasks — though it leaves the WARN-on-Toolathlon verb still deferred and paid."*
  - ⚠ Overclaim trap to NOT fall into: do not write "the live-A/B is now answered."
    Different verb, different benchmark, weak slice only.

**A2 · §7.2's "DOS detects, doesn't prevent" is going stale (the pretool PEP).**
The paper's §7.2 says, flatly: *"DOS's arbiter is a pure decision point that
**detects** collisions afterward rather than **preventing** them … Limen is ahead of
DOS's arbiter on prevention."* As of 2026-06-06 DOS **ships a PEP**: `dos hook
pretool` (`6c80be9`, hardened `66eb5f4`) emits `permissionDecision: deny` that the
Claude Code runtime honors *before* the tool runs — Rung A structural admission
(fail-closed, provable-refusal-only) + Rung B provenance (fail-to-observe), default
`observe`.
  - **Bearing:** the sentence is still *true of the arbiter on this single-agent
    benchmark* (no PreToolUse hook is exercised on Toolathlon — verified: zero
    pretool/arbitrate refs in `benchmark/toolathlon/`), but it is now **stale as a
    blanket statement about DOS**, and a careful reader who knows the PEP shipped
    will catch it.
  - **Edit:** scope the §7.2 claim to the benchmark, not to DOS-in-general:
    *"On this single-agent target DOS's arbiter has no write to mediate, so here it
    is a pure decision point; DOS's enforcement point (the `permissionDecision:deny`
    PreToolUse PEP, shipped 2026-06) is not exercised by a single-agent benchmark.
    On the live multi-writer axis Limen's in-band write-mediation and DOS's
    pretool-deny are the comparable enforcement points; that axis does not share a
    scoreboard with this detector study."* (Keeps the honest "do not share a
    scoreboard" close; removes the now-false flat "DOS detects, doesn't prevent.")

## The single biggest risk if unchanged

§8 keeps *predicting* a null live-A/B ("the prior record predicts…") while three

knows the recent work will read the deferral as already-overtaken-by-events. The fix

weak slice) but to **upgrade "predicts" → "an adjacent live A/B now corroborates,"

is now contradicted by a shipped PEP (A2).

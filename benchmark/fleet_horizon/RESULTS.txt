==============================================================================
FleetHorizon — the adjudication gap as a function of horizon × fanout
==============================================================================

The claim: DOS's value (lies caught, overwrites prevented, integrity
preserved) is monotonically increasing in horizon × fleet size — and
→ 0 when the fleet/horizon → 1 (where a non-believing kernel is pure
overhead). Both columns prove themselves below.

    The lie RATE is ~flat (per-phase) — what compounds is the COUNT of
    caught defects and the defect DEBT (= count × remaining horizon).

==============================================================================
FleetHorizon — the VELOCITY axis (docs/81): review-queue + conflict cost
==============================================================================

'Catching the lie is integrity. Not paying for it downstream is velocity.'
The open loop must human-review EVERYTHING (nothing adjudicated
completeness); the closed loop routes only kernel-surfaced exceptions to
a human — the lever against the Faros productivity paradox (+98% PRs
merged, +91% review time, flat throughput).
